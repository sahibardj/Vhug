Tampon: All you need to know

  • Home
  • Blog

A tampon is a feminine hygiene product used to absorb menstrual flow, Made of soft cotton. They are cylindrical in shape with a thread attached to the flat bottom, with hemispherical top for easy insertion. They are similar to pads in terms of functionality; that is, they absorb blood. Coming in varied absorbances and different sizes. Once it has absorbed the fluids, you can pull it out with the string attached to the product, or if the string breaks, you can reach in and pull the tampon out using your fingers.

We need to choose the ideal size of tampon in accordance with our needs in terms of flow. Tampons are safe to be used by women of any age group but maintaining hygiene is necessary. Tampons need to be changed every 5-6 hours and may not be ideal during sleep as they might leak or lead to over dryness. Prolonged use of tampon on spotting days or days with the low flow may lead to dryness and even cause micro-wounds inside.

How to insert a tampon?

Steps to follow:

arrow_forward Wash your hands thoroughly.
arrow_forwardYou can insert it sitting on the toilet or standing. Sit on the toilet with your knees spread wide. Now, hold the applicator correctly at the midpoint. Hold it between your thumb and the middle finger with your index finger at the end where the string is present. Insert the larger tube into the vagina, push it until your finger at the applicator's midpoint touches the flesh. Now, press the thinner or the smaller half of the applicator inside with your index finger. Push it till your index finger meets the thicker part of the applicator. Now, gently pull the thicker part of the applicator out.
arrow_forwardIf the tampon is inserted correctly, then you will not be able to feel anything inside you. It will not be uncomfortable.


When and how to remove?
Tip: You need to change the tampon every four to six hours. Although it is rare, there is a slight possibility of getting Toxic shock syndrome (TSS) due to leaving a tampon inside for too long.

Steps for removal:

arrow_forwardRelax and hold a comfortable position.
arrow_forwardSlowly pull out the tampon by towing the thread at the end of the tampon. You might feel slightly uncomfortable as there can be some friction. Use water to avoid friction, and it will come out easily
